Merger Timescale

Conselice used the CAS method to calculate the timescale of the merger process on dark-matter particle simulations, finding a merger timescale of ~0.3–0.8 Gyr for galaxies having a high-enough asymmetry to be identifie as an unambiguous merger.

This timescale is critical for interpreting galaxy merger fractions through cosmic time, as it allows us to convert merger fractions to merger rates and thus derive how mergers are driving galaxy formation. Using these results, Conselice (2006b) deduces that a typical galaxy undergoes $4.4^{+1.6}_{-0.9}$ mergers from z ~ 3 to z ~ 0.

In addition to giving a robust timescale, Conselice (2006b) also shows that only a fraction (about a third) of a merging galaxy’s time sequence would be identified as a major merger using CAS. The timescale derived is also largely independent of the viewing angle of the merger, and an asymmetry signal is only present within major mergers with mass ratios of 1:4 or greater.
