Merger Timescale

Conselice utilized the CAS approach to determine the merger timescale on dark-matter particle simulations and discovered that for galaxies with a merger period of $ sim 0.3-0.8$ Gyr, high enough asymmetry for the merging to be detected.

Since it enables us to translate galaxy merger fractions into merger rates and infer how mergers drive galaxy formation, this timeframe is essential for understanding galaxy merger fractions over cosmic time. Conselice (2006b) infers from these findings that a typical galaxy experiences $4.4^{+1.6}_{-0.9}$ mergers from $z \sim 3-0$.

Conselice (2006b) provides a reliable timescale and demonstrates that just a fraction (about a third) of a merging galaxy's time sequence would be recognized by CAS as a substantial merger. The period calculated is likewise generally independent of the merger's viewing angle, and only big mergers with mass ratios of 1:4 or above exhibit an asymmetry signal.

The quantitative structural characteristics detected when star formation and dust are included in models of galaxy structure are identical to those observed in neighboring galaxies. The measured structure correlates with other variables, such as color, in the same manner as it does for nearby galaxies.
